Orca Quick Facts

K33 backdive

  • Orcas are known as Killer Whales, but in fact they are dolphins. They are the largest members of the dolphin family.
  • Orcas live in clans, which consist of pods. Pods are matrilineal families with several matrilines within each pod.
  • This project focuses on the Southern Resident Killer Whale (SKRW) J clan that consists of J, K and L pods.
  • Orcas have distinct languages and dialects.

  • Orcas navigate the oceans by echolocation.
  • Males grow to an average of 20-26 feet and weigh 12,000 lb, and females grow to 18-22 feet and weigh between 8,000â€“11,000 lb.
  • Dorsal fins grow to be 6 feet all.
  • Calves weigh about 400 lb at birth and only about half survive the first year.
  • Orcas have a similar lifespan as humans.
  • SRKW population is at a 30 year low.
